## Intended use
Advisory ranking of available drivers for the most-urgent pending pickup request
in City2Cruise. The ranking is **advisory**: the dispatch layer applies it only
when `RL_ROUTING_ENABLED=true` and always falls back to geo-distance if the model
is slow/unavailable. Final oversight is human-in-the-loop via the Control Tower
(EU AI Act Art. 14).

## Training data
Fully **synthetic** episodes generated by `rl_service/train_tfm.py` with domain
randomization (Tobin et al., 2017). No personal or production data is used
(confidentiality annex). Reproducible: fixed seed 42, pinned dependencies.

## Limitations
- **Surpasses the greedy heuristic in the anticipatory formulation** (phase 2: event-driven cruise waves, driver contention, hold actions, hard all-aboard deadlines). In the phase-1 myopic formulation greedy was optimal by construction — documented in thesis §4.2 as the finding that motivated the environment redesign.
- The hold decision (waiting for a driver about to free up) is expressed in simulation; the serving path exposes an advisory ranking, where a hold surfaces as ranking a soon-to-free driver above distant free ones.
- Sim-to-real fidelity is measured against representative reference fixtures, not
  live production telemetry (future work).
- Trained on synthetic data only.

## Regulatory considerations (EU Reg. 2024/1689)
- **Art. 12 record-keeping** — predictions logged to `rl_inference_log`; runs tracked in MLflow.
- **Art. 14 human oversight** — advisory output + Control Tower manual intervention + geo fallback.
- **Art. 15 accuracy/robustness** — release gate (convergence/fidelity/robustness) + drift monitoring.
- **Art. 17 quality management** — versioned registry, promotion gate, reproducible CI training.
